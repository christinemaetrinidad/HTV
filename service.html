Hematology

The science or study of blood and blood disorders is known as hematology. Hematology is the study and practice of treating diseases of the blood, including sickle cell anemia, leukemia, lymphoma, myeloma, and many kinds of hemophilia. The physiology, pathology, etiology, diagnosis, treatment, prognosis, and prevention of blood-related illnesses are all covered under the field of internal medicine known as hematology.

Clinical Chemistry

Clinical chemistry is the study of the biochemistry of bodily fluids. The concentrations of different chemical components in body fluids are measured via chemical reactions. The most often tested specimens in clinical chemistry, blood and urine, can contain a variety of chemicals that can be detected and quantified using a number of straightforward chemical assays.

Blood Chemistry

a procedure to evaluate the presence of certain chemicals in the body using a sample of blood. These substances include lipids, proteins, glucose (sugar), enzymes, and electrolytes (such as sodium, potassium, and chloride). Important information on a person's kidney, liver, and other organ functions may be obtained through blood chemistry testing. An excessive concentration of a chemical in the blood may indicate a sickness or be a side effect of medication. Numerous illnesses may be diagnosed and monitored using blood chemistry testing before, during, and after therapy. also known as a blood chemical analysis.

Serology

A blood test to look for antibodies to a particular microbe. Any exposure to a specific bacterium can be detected with a serologic test.

Chemistry

Chemistry panels are collections of tests that are frequently requested to assess a person's general state of health. They aid in assessing things like the electrolyte balance of the body and/or the health of numerous important organs. A blood sample is used for the testing, which is typically taken from a vein.

Electrolytes

An electrolyte test is a blood test that determines whether the body has an electrolyte imbalance. Electrolytes are ions and minerals present in the blood, such as sodium, potassium, chloride, and bicarbonate. They have the ability to carry electrical impulses throughout the body.

Enzymes

An Enzyme Marker Test, also known as enzyme analysis, is a blood test that measures the activity of certain enzymes in a sample of blood serum, often to discover a hereditary illness or aberrant state that might cause these enzymes to cease operating or become inefficient.

Hepatitis Profile

The hepatitis virus panel is a set of blood tests used to detect present or previous hepatitis A, B, or C infection. It can analyze blood samples for many hepatitis viruses at the same time. Each of the hepatitis viruses may be detected using antibody and antigen assays.

Tumor Markers

A test that determines the presence of tumor markers in tissue, blood, urine, or other bodily fluids. Most tumor markers are proteins produced by both normal and cancer cells, but cancer cells produce more of them.
